Brand-Facing Dashboard (what each customer sees)

B2B sales lives or dies on whether brands can prove ROI here.

Engagement metrics (per Polst):

Total responses
Response velocity (responses/hour, peak detection)
Completion rate (started vs. submitted)
Drop-off point (which question lost them)
Geographic distribution of responses
Device/channel breakdown (web, embed, widget, social)
Time-to-first-response and time-to-statistical-significance


Audience composition (per Polst and aggregated):

Demographic split — age, gender, location to city level
Returning vs. new respondents
Cross-Polst behavior — did this respondent answer your other Polsts?
Audience overlap with category (e.g., “your respondents also voted on these top 5 Polsts”)


Decision intelligence (the layer that justifies the price):

Confidence interval / statistical significance flag
Sentiment shift over time (if Polst is rerun)
Geographic heatmap with click-through to city-level results
Comparative benchmarks — your result vs. category average
“What changed” alerts — sudden shifts in sentiment


Campaign-level (across multiple Polsts):

Total reach, total responses, cost per response
Top-performing Polst by engagement
Audience growth over time
Question library / template performance


Export and integration:

CSV/JSON export of raw response data
API access tier (enterprise)
Webhook for real-time response events
BI tool connectors (Looker, Tableau, PowerBI) — this matters for enterprise
